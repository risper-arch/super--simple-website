Economist Ezekiel Muguna

History

Ezekiel muguna(born in september, 19, 2002) is a kenyan economist and a statistician who received the 2021 nobel prize in economic sciences for his research on consumption analysis, monetary history and theory and complexity of stabilization policy. With John Nyamai was among the leaders in school of economics in Meru. He associated with the faculty in the school and rejected corruption. he recruited several students, young professors and academics to be leading economists. He is now working as a retired economists in one of his macroeconomics office in Mombasa.

Education

Ezekiel gained his degrees and doctorates certificates at Laikipia university. He further went for phd in Rutgers University. During his education life he was able to do the following research : Monetarism, NUTURAL STATE OF UNEMPLOYMENT,CONSUMPTION IN ECONOMICS and FINANCIAL CRISIS.Ezekiel also wrote books about economics. They were titled : "THE ECONOMIST" and ' GROW RICH'.

Experiences

Ezekiel experience has alot of contribution in the economy. He decsribed A THEORY OF THE CONSUMPTION FUNTION as articulation of permanent income hypothesis, the idea that a household consumption and savings decisions are more affected by changes in its permanent income than by income changes that household members perceive as tempolary or transitory. The permanent income hypothesis provided an explanations for some puzzles that had emerged in the impirical data concerning the relationship between the average and merginal prospensities to consume. it also helped to explain why for example, fiscal policy in the form of tax increase, if perceived as tempolary, might npt lead to inteded reductions in consumptions; instead, the increased tax might be financed out of savings, leaving consumption levels unchanged. That was Ezekiels experience and finding too.
